Case Study: Social Engineering
& Account Takeover Prevention

Financial Crime Account Takeover Social Engineering KYC Verification Process Adherence

Identifying a fraudster impersonating a legitimate customer to gain unauthorised account access

Overview

During a live support interaction, I identified and neutralised a social engineering attempt targeting a customer's fintech account. The fraudster attempted to exploit an unverified support channel to force an email address change — a classic account takeover technique. Through strict process adherence, behavioural risk awareness, and proactive follow-up, I prevented unauthorised access and secured the account with zero financial loss.

01 — Situation

An unregistered contact reached the chat support channel claiming to be a customer locked out of their account. They requested an immediate email address change, citing urgency around a pending financial transaction. When asked to complete standard identity verification, the individual became hostile — refusing to cooperate, demanding escalation, and applying social pressure to bypass the process. No manager was available at the time.

02 — Risk Signals Identified

Several behavioural red flags were present: contact via an unregistered channel, refusal to cooperate with standard verification, artificial urgency created around a financial transaction, and hostility that escalated the moment identity confirmation was requested. These are consistent patterns in social engineering and account takeover fraud.

03 — Action Taken

I maintained strict process adherence under pressure and declined to make any account changes without full verification. After the individual disconnected, I proactively contacted the legitimate registered account holder directly by phone. I completed enhanced multi-point verification — including recent transaction history and previously transacted recipient details — going beyond basic demographic checks to confirm I was speaking with the real customer.

04 — Discovery & Resolution

During the call, multiple failed login attempts were identified against the customer's registered email — confirming the earlier contact had been a fraudster. The customer had no awareness their account was being targeted. The account was secured immediately, email address updated with the verified customer, and they were advised on digital safety practices including avoiding unknown third-party links and protecting personal email credentials.

✓

Outcome: Account takeover prevented with zero financial loss. The legitimate customer's account was fully secured and they were educated on protective measures. The customer later sent a formal written appreciation for the handling of the case.

Key learning: Genuine customers cooperate with verification. Resistance, urgency, and hostility are risk signals — not reasons to bypass process. Recognising this behavioural pattern and holding firm without managerial support was the decisive factor in preventing the fraud.
